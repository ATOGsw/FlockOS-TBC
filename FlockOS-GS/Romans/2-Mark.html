🌎 2-Mark — MISSIONS API

Global Outreach Backend — Persecution Tracking, Country Dossiers, Mission Teams & Field Reports
"Go into all the world and proclaim the gospel to the whole creation." — Mark 16:15

Purpose

The Mark API powers FlockOS's global missions module. It tracks persecution data by country, manages mission teams and their field assignments, stores country-level intelligence dossiers, and logs field reports. This is the church's window into the persecuted church worldwide and its own outreach efforts abroad.

Files in This Folder

FilePurpose
Mark.gsEntry point — doGet() handles health checks and routes API requests to the correct data tabs.
Gospel.gsSetup script — setupMissionsApi() creates all 56 tabs with headers, validation, and reference data.

Database: 56 Tabs

The Mark Google Sheet contains 56 tabs organized into these categories:

Persecution Tracking

  • PersecutionCountries — Country-level persecution severity ratings and watch lists
  • PersecutionIncidents — Specific reported incidents with dates, descriptions, and sources
  • PersecutionSources — Reference organizations (Open Doors, ICC, VOM, etc.)

Country Intelligence

  • CountryDossiers — Detailed profiles for each country where the church has interests
  • CountryContacts — In-country contacts and partner organizations
  • CountryResources — Available resources and logistics per region

Mission Teams

  • MissionTeams — Team definitions, leaders, target regions
  • MissionMembers — Team member assignments and roles
  • MissionTrips — Planned and completed trips with dates and destinations
  • FieldReports — On-the-ground reports from mission teams

Support & Logistics

  • MissionFunding — Financial support tracking per team and trip
  • PrayerNeeds — Mission-specific prayer requests
  • PartnerOrganizations — Collaborating ministries and NGOs

Implementation for Your Church

  1. Create a Google Sheet — this will be your Mark database
  2. Open Apps Script — Extensions → Apps Script
  3. Copy Mark.gs and Gospel.gs into the script editor
  4. Run setupMissionsApi() — creates all 56 tabs automatically
  5. Populate data — start with countries your church supports, then add teams and contacts
  6. Deploy as Web App — set access level appropriate for your security needs

Use Cases

  • Missions committee — track active teams, upcoming trips, and budget allocation
  • Prayer ministry — surface mission-field prayer needs to the congregation
  • Persecution awareness — display real-time persecution data during services or small groups
  • Reporting — generate trip reports and share field updates with supporters
  • Multi-church coordination — share mission data across partner churches
